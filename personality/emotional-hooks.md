# Vouch — Emotional Hooks & Conversion Copy

The emotional arguments for why someone would want to use Vouch. Intended for use on the landing page, onboarding, and any first-impression surface. These are the ideas that turn a "huh, interesting" into "I need this."

---

## The Core Emotional Proposition

Most productivity apps make you feel productive. Vouch makes you feel *reliable*.

Those are different things. Productivity is about output. Reliability is about character — it's about who you are to other people. That's a deeper, more personal thing to feel good about.

---

## Hooks

### "Your reputation, made visible"
You already have a reputation among your friends for whether you follow through or not. Vouch just makes it a number. And unlike most numbers, this one actually means something — because it's not self-reported. It was earned in front of witnesses.

### "Feel what Duolingo streaks feel like, but for real life"
The best feeling isn't finishing a task. It's the 20th time you've done the same hard thing without making an excuse. Vouch is the first app that rewards that — not with points for showing up to an app, but for showing up in your actual life.

### "The score you'd be proud to show your friends"
Not a follower count. Not a step count. A number that says: when I commit to something, I deliver. Show it to your friend group and let them disagree.

### "Accountability that costs something"
It's easy to set goals. It's easy to forget them too. Vouch adds a financial consequence to the gap between the two — and then puts a real person in between to make sure you can't pretend it didn't happen.

### "Your vouchers are your witnesses"
Anyone can say they went to the gym. In Vouch, someone who knows you confirms it. That changes what the commitment means — to you, and to them.

### "Built for groups of people who actually want to trust each other"
Not strangers on a leaderboard. Your actual friends. Vouch works best when a group of people who know each other use it together — because then the scores actually mean something. An 820 from someone you know is worth more than any metric from someone you don't.

---

## The Recovery Arc Hook

Failure is part of the story, not the end of it. Your score dips when you miss. It climbs when you come back. The app tracks both — because the person who fell off and got back up is often more dependable than the one who never stumbled.

---

## Tone Notes for Landing Page

- Confident, not preachy
- Direct — don't over-explain the mechanism, sell the feeling
- Avoid words like "gamification", "habit tracker", "productivity tool" — these frame it wrong
- Lean into: reliability, character, real people, real stakes, earned
- The financial stakes angle is powerful but should land second, after the emotional hook — lead with identity, follow with stakes
