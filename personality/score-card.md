# Vouch Score Card

A shareable, visual summary of a user's reputation — designed to be something people *want* to show off. Think Spotify Wrapped, but for personal reliability.

---

## Concept

At any point (monthly, annually, or on-demand), a user can generate a score card that captures their accountability snapshot. The card is beautiful, concise, and shareable to social media or messaging apps.

The key differentiator from any other shareable stat card: **"Verified by real people."** Every number on this card was witnessed and confirmed by actual humans in the user's life — not self-reported, not algorithmic. That's what makes it mean something.

---

## Card Contents

- **Reputation score** — large, prominent, with tier label (e.g. "812 — Solid Rep")
- **Longest recurring streak** — e.g. "42-day gym streak"
- **Tasks delivered** — total completed on time
- **Money kept** — total financial stakes *not* donated to charity, reframed positively as "you followed through on €X worth of commitments"
- **Verified by** — number of unique vouchers who confirmed your work
- **Score trajectory** — a small sparkline showing score over the period

---

## Framing & Copy

The card should feel like an achievement, not a report card. Suggested headline formats:
- "I delivered." (simple, confident)
- "842. Built over 6 months." (score + time = earned)
- "Verified by 4 people who know me." (social proof angle)

---

## Strategic Value

- **Organic marketing** — people sharing cards brings Vouch to new audiences without paid acquisition
- **Pride without bragging** — the card lets the score speak, the user doesn't have to
- **Real-world credibility** — a sharable artifact that represents genuine character, not app engagement

---

## Notes

- Cards should be visually distinct and on-brand — dark theme, VFD/retro-cyber aesthetic consistent with the app
- Consider annual "Vouch Wrapped" as a yearly ritual moment, in addition to on-demand cards
- This is a marketing surface as much as a product feature — design and copy decisions here should be treated with that lens
